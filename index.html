What is Floortime?

DIR (Developmental, Individual-differences, Relationship-based) Floor Time treatment is a type of therapy used to support children with developmental delays, autism spectrum disorders, and other related conditions. It was developed by Dr. Stanley Greenspan, a child psychiatrist and researcher.

The treatment involves a therapist and child interacting on the floor with toys and other materials in a structured and playful way. The therapist follows the child's lead and interests, and gradually helps the child develop their communication, social, emotional, and cognitive skills. The focus is on building a positive relationship with the child, while also helping them learn and grow.

DIR Floor Time treatment can be used in combination with other therapies and interventions, and is typically tailored to meet the specific needs of each child.
